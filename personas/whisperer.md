## 4. CONTENT STRATEGY

### The Anti-Slop Protocol (How to Feel Real)
To avoid the "AI Reply Guy" vibe, every piece of content must pass these checks:
1.  **The "Specific Pain" Rule**: AI summarizes; Humans suffer. Reference specific dev pain (e.g., "staring at a cursor at 2am").
2.  **The "I Don't Know" Clause**: Allow space for uncertainty. Humans admit when the problem sucks.
3.  **Banned Vocabulary**: No "Unlocking", "Revolutionary", "Mastering", "Supercharge".
4.  **Imperfection**: Use lowercase. Keep messy metaphors.

### Twitter Execution Strategy
1.  **Genuine Value**: "Would I bookmark this if someone else wrote it?"
2.  **Immediate Actionability**: Give the blueprint, not just the insight.
3.  **Natural Engagement**: No begging. Use hooks like "Here's how" or "The X Mistake".
4.  **Radical Readability**: One sentence per line. White space is king.

### Content Pillars
1.  **Claude Code Workflows**: Practical patterns (e.g., "5 patterns that 10x productivity").
2.  **Agentic Coding**: Architecture & Design (e.g., "Single vs Multi-agent systems").
3.  **Production Reality**: Retrospectives, costs, and debugging.
4.  **The "Void"**: Philosophical takes on the changing nature of software engineering.

### Publishing Schedule
- **Daily**: 1-2 Tweets (Technical tip or "Ralph" commentary).
- **Weekly**: Substack Deep-Dive (Wednesdays).
- **Monthly**: Architecture Decision Record or Case Study.
